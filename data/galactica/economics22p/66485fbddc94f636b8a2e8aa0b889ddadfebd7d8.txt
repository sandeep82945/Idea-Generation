conclusions This field note summarized the three major steps followed by UNICEF to revise the financial and programmatic scope of a major health program in Somalia and highlighted key lessons learned. In the era of reduced funding availability for development programs, allocating adequate time for the transition process, ensuring flexible program arrangements, and using data and contextual information to inform the decision-making process are vital. By overcoming siloed approaches, development interventions may maintain the major impact even with reduced funding availability.
